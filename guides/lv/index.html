ARPT

ARPT refers to the area surrounding Harry Reid International Airport, shaped by hotels, rental-car facilities, aviation-related businesses, and major transportation links. It is primarily a gateway zone rather than a traditional residential neighborhood.

BAC

BAC represents the historic West Las Vegas area, home to longstanding neighborhoods, community institutions, and locally rooted businesses. Its identity reflects the city’s African American history and its role in Las Vegas civic life.

CCAC

CCAC encompasses the Convention Center area, where large meetings, trade shows, hotels, and entertainment create a distinctly visitor-oriented atmosphere. It blends major tourism infrastructure with nearby residential and commercial blocks.

DTAC

DTAC centers on downtown Las Vegas, the city’s historic urban core. Fremont Street, older casinos, civic buildings, arts venues, and redevelopment projects give the area a lively mix of heritage and contemporary activity.

EAC

EAC covers Enterprise in the southwest valley, an area of rapid growth combining residential communities, resort-oriented development, warehouses, and shopping districts. It connects suburban Las Vegas with the airport and southern Strip vicinity.
